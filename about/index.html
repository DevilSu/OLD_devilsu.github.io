About

Yang-Hsi Su

A fourth year (yeah yeah yeah I know it's taking a while) Ph.D. student at University of Michigan in Electrical and Computer Engineering, advised by Prof. Alanson Sample.

I am a member of the Interactive Sensing and Computing Lab along with Yasha Iravantchi, Tom Krolikowski, Kunpeng Huang, Cameron Haire, and Yaxuan Li.

(Shout out to former labmates Amani Alkayyali and Sumukh Marathe as well!)

My research mainly focuses on RF sensing for indoor localization and/or human activity recognition.

As a die-hard fan of Doraemon, my ultimate goal is to have a forth-dimension pocket that has all kinds of cool gadgets like the one Doraemon has.

Projects

  • Cool BLE switched antenna array to track the AoA of BLE AD packets
  • 60GHz SFCW radar (still working on it lol, but its really cool)
  • PoseID - pose reconstruction with RFID tags
  • TomoID: A Scalable Approach to Device Free Indoor Localization via RFID Tomography, INFOCOM 2023, May @ NYC, my first paper :)
